# DeepGem SOW Template

## Structure

The SOW has these sections in order:

1. **Cover Page** - Client name, company, date, prepared by
2. **Opening Letter** - Personalized to the client, references the conversation
3. **Project Overview** - What we're building and why (2-3 paragraphs)
4. **Key Deliverables** - Bullet list of what this engagement produces
5. **Definition of Done** - Summary table + detailed epics with acceptance criteria
6. **What's Out of Scope** - Explicit boundaries
7. **Investment & Timeline** - Hours, rate, total, retainer, dates
8. **How We Work** - Billing, payment, communication cadence
9. **Terms & Conditions** - Standard legal (mostly boilerplate)
10. **Agreement** - Signature block

## Opening Letter Template

```
[CLIENT FIRST NAME],

Thank you for choosing DeepGem Interactive. We're excited to partner with you on this
project and are committed to delivering results that make a real difference for your
business.

This document is designed to be straightforward. It covers what we're building together,
how we'll know it's done, and the practical terms of our engagement. No surprises—just
clarity.

Here's what you'll find:

1. Project Overview — What we're building and why
2. Definition of Done — Clear success criteria so we're aligned
3. What's Out of Scope — Boundaries that protect us both
4. Investment & Timeline — Hours, costs, and schedule
5. Terms & Conditions — The fine print (it's actually pretty short)

Our approach is collaborative. We'll be in regular communication, and if anything changes
or questions come up, we'll address them together. That's how we do our best work.

Let's build something great.

Warmly,

Ziah

Founder & CEO, DeepGem Interactive
```

**Customization guidance:** When the conversation had specific emotional resonance or budget constraints, personalize the opening. Example from EfficientAid:

> "I continue to feel deep resonance with your mission of creating the best-in-class executive assistant company in the world. I want to support you in this vision and your exit. I know the times are tight right now, and so we worked hard to come up with a solution that is budget conscious..."

## Terms & Conditions (Boilerplate)

These are standard and rarely change:

### Billing & Payment
1. 20% retainer due before work begins, applied to final invoice
2. Bill for actual hours at agreed rate (estimates are not caps unless "not-to-exceed")
3. Invoices bi-weekly, due within 15 days
4. Late payments: 1.5% monthly interest, may pause work at 30+ days overdue

### Scope Changes
5. Changes require written agreement before work begins
6. Proactive communication if trending over estimate

### Ownership
7. Client owns deliverables upon full payment
8. DeepGem retains rights to pre-existing tools/frameworks (client gets license)

### Warranties & Liability
9. Work will meet agreed Definition of Done
10. AI systems can behave unexpectedly - build to criteria but can't guarantee all scenarios
11. Liability limited to fees paid; no indirect damages

### Confidentiality
12. Mutual confidentiality, survives 3 years

### Termination
13. Either party can end with 30 days notice
14. Pay for completed work + 2-week transition fee if mid-project
15. Deliver all completed work upon final payment

### Legal
16. Colorado law governs, Denver courts
17. Independent contractors
18. No-hire clause: 1 year after project
